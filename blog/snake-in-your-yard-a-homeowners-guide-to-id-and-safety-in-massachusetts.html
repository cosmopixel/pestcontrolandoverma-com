Common Snake Species in Andover, Massachusetts

Massachusetts is home to several snake species, most of which are harmless. Here are the most common snakes you might encounter in your Andover yard:

  • Garter Snake: Most common, with distinctive stripes
  • Northern Water Snake: Often mistaken for water moccasins
  • Eastern Milk Snake: Beneficial for rodent control
  • Ring-necked Snake: Small and shy

Identifying Massachusetts' Only Venomous Snake

The Timber Rattlesnake is Massachusetts' only venomous snake, and it's extremely rare in residential areas. If you're concerned about snake identification in North Andover or surrounding areas, don't hesitate to contact our wildlife experts.

Key Features of the Timber Rattlesnake:

  • Diamond-shaped head
  • Distinctive rattle at tail end
  • Dark bands across a brown or gray body
  • Typically 3-5 feet long

Making Your Yard Less Attractive to Snakes

Prevention is key when it comes to snake control. Similar to our approach with mole control in Andover, managing your yard's environment can significantly reduce snake presence:

  • Remove brush, wood piles, and debris
  • Keep grass short and well-maintained
  • Seal gaps around foundations
  • Address rodent problems (consider our carpenter ant control services)
  • Install proper fencing

What to Do If You Encounter a Snake

If you spot a snake in your yard:

  • Remain calm and slowly back away
  • Keep children and pets at a safe distance
  • Never attempt to handle or kill the snake
  • Contact professional wildlife control if needed

Just as we discuss in our guide about dealing with wildlife in your home, professional handling ensures everyone's safety.

When to Call Professional Snake Control

While most snake encounters in Lawrence and Andover are harmless, professional intervention may be necessary if:

  • You can't identify the snake species
  • The snake is in your home or basement
  • You're experiencing repeated snake encounters
  • You suspect a snake den on your property
